About

Electronic Music Atlas is a resource website that introduces underground electronic music through genre, DJ culture, and atmosphere. Rather than treating music as fixed categories, this project maps it as a network of scenes, energy, and influence.

Purpose

This website was created as an accessible entry point for people who are curious about electronic music but may not know where to begin. It introduces different styles in a way that feels visual, direct, and easy to explore.

Focus

The project focuses on underground and club-based electronic music cultures, including gabber, psytrance, techno, hardcore, and drum & bass. It highlights how sound is shaped not only by genre, but also by performance, space, and subculture.

Design Direction

The visual system uses moving backgrounds, dark tones, and immersive media to reflect the dreamlike, intense, and atmospheric qualities of electronic music scenes. The goal is to make the website feel like an experience rather than only a collection of facts.

Creator Statement

I wanted to show how genres connect and how DJs help shape different scenes. Instead of presenting electronic music as something fixed, I wanted to treat it as something living, emotional, and constantly moving between spaces, people, and identities.

This site combines information with atmosphere so that users can not only read about electronic music, but also get a stronger feeling for its visual and cultural energy.
