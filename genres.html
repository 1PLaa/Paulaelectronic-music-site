Electronic Music Genres

Electronic music is not just one sound. It moves through clubs, warehouses, festivals, and underground scenes in very different ways. Hover over each card to experience a moving preview of each genre’s atmosphere.

Gabber

Hardcore / Aggressive / Raw

Gabber is a fast and intense form of hardcore electronic music that became strongly associated with rave culture in the Netherlands. Its distorted kicks and relentless energy create a harsh, physical listening experience.

The genre feels direct, rebellious, and industrial. It is often connected to underground parties, warehouse spaces, and a more extreme side of club culture.

Psytrance

Hypnotic / Psychedelic / Festival

Psytrance is known for its repetitive rhythms, layered textures, and immersive atmosphere. It often builds a trance-like feeling through fast beats and swirling electronic sounds.

This genre is closely linked to outdoor festivals and collective experience. Its sound feels cosmic, spiritual, and dreamlike while still keeping strong dance energy.

Drum & Bass

Fast / Bass-heavy / Urban

Drum & Bass combines rapid breakbeats with deep basslines, creating a sound that is both energetic and highly rhythmic. It can feel dark and atmospheric or sharp and explosive depending on the track.

The genre has a strong club presence and a distinct urban intensity. It is designed to move quickly, hit hard, and keep the listener locked into momentum.
